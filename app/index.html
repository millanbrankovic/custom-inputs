Pure CSS Custom Inputs

Default checkboxes and radio buttons are quite plain and their appearance can't be easily customized - at least it was the case in the past.
Luckily, CSS offers a good solution - :checked pseudo class.

With this approach, we avoid the use of JavaScript in order to add "loolike appearance", attach click event handlers, hide original inputs etc.

Nowadays, SVG support is very good and can be used for various design tweaks and may be combined with ::before & ::after pseudo selectors and thus get a nice final "look".
This is pretty safe to use on any project and much easier with PostCSS & cssnext ;)
